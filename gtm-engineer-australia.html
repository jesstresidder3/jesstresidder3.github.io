What a GTM engineer does

GTM stands for go-to-market. A GTM engineer builds the systems that handle the repetitive parts of sales, marketing and operations, so the early team can spend its time on the conversations that actually close, not on copying names into a spreadsheet at 9pm.

For most startups the alternative is hiring an SDR, then another, then a tool nobody fully sets up. I build the pipeline with AI instead, so the work scales without the headcount climbing with it.

What I build

  • Prospect research that pulls a real picture of each account, not just a name and a title.
  • List building and enrichment that fills in the gaps automatically and keeps the data clean.
  • Personalised outreach sequences that read like you wrote them, not like a mail merge.
  • CRM automation so records update themselves and nothing falls through.
  • Reporting that tells you what's working without you building the spreadsheet.

I've built this from scratch

I built the entire go-to-market and sales system at ReubenAI, a B2B AI SaaS startup, starting with nothing: prospect research, list building, enrichment, outreach sequencing, CRM automation, and the content pipeline behind it. So when I set this up for you, it's from someone who has run the whole thing, not just read about it.

B2B founders and early-stage startups who want a proper pipeline built with AI, from first list to booked meeting. We can work as a one-off audit, a full build, or an ongoing retainer, depending on how much you want handled.
